6. Nigerian Fried Eggs with Bread

Ingredients:

  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 tomato (diced)
  • 1/4 onion (sliced)
  • 1 scotch bonnet (chopped, optional)
  • Salt and seasoning to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Bread (sliced)

Preparation:

  1. Beat eggs in a bowl with salt and seasoning.
  2. Stir in the chopped tomato, onion, and pepper.
  3. Heat oil in a pan and pour in the egg mixture.
  4. Fry on medium heat until set.
  5. Serve with bread.

8. Akara (Bean Cakes)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup peeled beans (or beans flour)
  • 1/2 onion (chopped)
  • 1 scotch bonnet (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Preparation:

  1. Blend peeled beans with little water, pepper, and onion until thick.
  2. Stir in salt.
  3. Heat oil in a frying pan. Scoop batter into hot oil in small balls.
  4. Fry until golden brown on both sides.
  5. Drain and serve with pap, custard, bread, or garri water.

9. Custard or Pap with Akara or Moi Moi

Ingredients:

  • Custard powder or akamu/pap
  • Sugar (optional)
  • Milk or evaporated milk
  • Hot water

Preparation:

  1. Mix custard powder with cold water to form a smooth paste.
  2. Pour in boiling water while stirring continuously until it thickens.
  3. Add sugar and milk if desired.
  4. Serve with akara or moi moi.

11. Yam Porridge (Asaro) â€” Fast Cooking Style

Ingredients:

  • 1 tomato and 1 pepper (blended)

12. Ewa Agoyin (Mashed Beans with Spicy Sauce)

Ingredients for Beans:

  • 1 cup brown or black-eyed beans
  • Salt

Ingredients for Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup palm oil
  • 2 dry peppers (blended)
  • 1 small onion (chopped)
  • Salt and seasoning cube

Preparation:

  1. Boil beans until very soft and mash slightly.
  2. For sauce, bleach palm oil until smoky, reduce heat.
  3. Add onions and fry until almost burnt.
  4. Add blended dry pepper, season, and fry until dark.
  5. Serve beans with the spicy oil sauce.

13. Abacha (African Salad) â€” Quick Version

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups abacha (soaked in warm water and drained)
  • 1/4 cup palm oil
  • 1 tsp ground crayfish
  • 1 tsp potash (dissolved in water, optional)
  • Pepper and onion (sliced)
  • Ugba (oil bean, optional)
  • Seasoning cube and salt
  • Garden eggs (optional)

Preparation:

  1. Mix palm oil and potash water until it thickens and turns yellowish.
  2. Add crayfish, pepper, onions, ugba, salt, and seasoning.
  3. Stir in soaked abacha until evenly coated.
  4. Garnish and serve with garden eggs or fish.
